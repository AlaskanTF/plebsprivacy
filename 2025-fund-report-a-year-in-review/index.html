2025 Performance Review

The fund delivered a 61.89% return in 2025, improving meaningfully on 2024 returns and bringing the cumulative return since inception to ~351%. The results were achieved during a year marked by sharp market swings and elevated uncertainty.

The most challenging period was a 26.27% drawdown, primarily triggered by unexpected tariff announcements that caused a rapid and broad based sell off in the market. While the decline was sudden, decisions were made not with emotions. Positions were reassessed rationally, premiums were made through selling of puts at peak panic, free cash and premiums were allocated, risk was managed and the portfolio was allowed to recover as volatility normalised.

2025 reinforced a core principle: process matters more than prediction.

Strategy and Execution

The strategy throughout the year remained consistent:

  • Accumulate high-quality U.S. equities
  • Selling cash-secured puts and covered calls
  • Reinvesting option premiums and realised gains to compound capital

The approach worked particularly well in a volatile environment. Higher implied volatility increase option premiums and rather than treating this income defensively, profits were actively redeployed to compound capital.

What Went Right

  • Disciplined accumulation: Option premiums were consistently reinvest rather than sidelined.
  • Emotional control: Volatile markets tested patience, but reactionary decisions were avoided.
  • Active engagement: Staying close to the market allowed for better sizing and positioning.

What Could Have Been Better

  • Over-hedging: Hedges were maintained longer than necessary in a bull market, increasing cost and reducing returns.
  • Execution hesitation: In several cases, research conclusions were correct but hesitation prevented capital deployment leading to lowered returns.

These lessons will directly inform future decision-making.

Market Environment

2025 was shaped by policy uncertainty, tariff related headlines and quick sentiment shifts. Rather than attempting to predict or forecast macro outcomes, the focus remained on managing exposure rationally and using volatility as an input and not a reason to abandon the planned strategy.

Outlook for 2026

Heading into 2025, the outlook is cautiously optimistic

Markets may continue to perform reasonably well but likely at a slower pace with ongoing volatility. The core strategy will remained unchanged, with a potential increment in allocation towards consumer staples as we monitor the cycles.

The goal remains to stay risk-aware without being blinded by short-term scares.

Closing Thoughts

Since inception in 2023, the fund has compounded approx ~351% but not without volatility, mistakes or uncomfortable periods. This focus remains on maintaining a repeatable process, disciplined risk management and long-term compounding.
