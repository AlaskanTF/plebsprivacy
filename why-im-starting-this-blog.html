Why I’m Starting This Blog

August 4, 2025 AlaskanTF(ATF)
Privacy Journey Reflections Travel Journal

Hello there again,

This must be the 10th time I’m making a “Why I’m Starting This Blog” post.
At this point, it’s practically tradition.

Every time I’ve started a blog, it was because something felt worth documenting—some shift in how I live, think, or move through the world. This time, two big themes are pulling me in: my journey toward better digital privacy and my travel experiences.

Why privacy?

Over the past few years, I’ve become increasingly aware of just how much of my digital life is tracked, monetized, and centralized. I don’t like it. I want more control, less noise, and fewer middlemen between me and the tools I use.

I’ve started taking steps—changing browsers, rethinking my phone, exploring alternative platforms—but it’s a messy process. So I figured: why not journal it here?

Why travel?

Travel has always been part of how I reset and reconnect. Whether it’s a short trip out of town or something longer and off-grid, I notice that being in new environments challenges my routines (in a good way).

It also brings up new privacy questions:
How do I book without giving up too much info?
How do I take photos and share memories without bleeding metadata?

Why write it down?

Because writing helps me commit.
Because someone else might find it useful.
Because I’ve tried (and failed) to make something like this stick before, and that’s okay.

Maybe this version is the one that lasts. Or maybe it isn’t. But here it is—post #1.

Let’s see where it goes.
